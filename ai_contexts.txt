
kimi_mtr.js:

Focus on the @kimi_mtr directory, specifically @kimi_mtr/kimi_mtr.html, @kimi_mtr/kimi_mtr.js, and @kimi_mtr/animation-generator.js in this task.
This is an animation generator that generates time-lapse animations showing subway trains moving on a map. Each train is marked by a label and moves around.

It takes in a javascript array called "lines". In the array, each object is a "line" with a specific color, name, and marker type. There are "branches" in each "line" with a specific spawn frequency and a list of stations that represent branches of the line. Each station contains a latitude and longtitude, travel time, dwell time, and an optional list of checkpoints. The travel time is the time, in seconds, needed for the train to travel to the next station on the list. The dwell time is the time, in seconds, that the train stops at the station. The list of checkpoints contains checkpoints that the train passes when going to the next station. Each checkpoint has a latitude and longtitude, and a "progress" value between 0 and 1 representing the amount of progress the train makes when it goes pass the checkpoint. For instance, if the "progress" is 0.7, then the train passes through the checkpoint when it is 70% of the way to the next station.

The simulation is generated in the "generation" process. In this process, for each branch of each line, the program generates an array containing the coordinates (latitude and longtitude) of a train on the branch. The nth entry in the array stores the coordinates of the train in the nth second from the beginning (relative to when the train started). The length of the array is equal to the time the train takes to take a roundtrip to and from the starting point. Specifically, the train starts at the first station in the array. It goes station by station, following the run and dwell times specified. Once it reaches the last station of the branch, it turns around and goes back to the starting point. When it goes back to where it started, it has completed a roundtrip. Note that for branches of branch_type "circular", trains do not turn around. Instead, when they reach the end, they go on to the first stop, and complete their round trip then.

During the "playing" section, the program plays an animation of trains moving, with markers representing trains on the map. Each train has a "progress" value which is the progress, in seconds, of the train on the route. If the train's progress is in the nth second, then the train's coordinates are in the nth position of the array generated in the generation process. The starting progresses of the trains are determined by the spawn frequency. Trains when initialized, are spaced out by spawn frequencies across the time taken for a round trip. For instance, if the spawn frequency is 100 seconds per train and the round trip time is 350 seconds, then there will be trains starting at 0, 100, and 200 seconds respectively. There should be no train at 300 seconds as the 300 second train would be just 50 seconds from the 0 train. It's better to have too few trains than to have too many trains, so there shouldn't be a train at 300 seconds. 

The simulation runs with one frame marking one second in the simulation. Every frame, the time progress of each train on each branch adds by one. In the previous example, the trains would be at 1, 101, and 201 seconds respectively after one second. If the time progress of a train exceeds the round trip time, then the position of the train should be the remainder of the time progress divided by the round trip time.
